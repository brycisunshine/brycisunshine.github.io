Things I Believe

My philosophy —
such as it is.

Not rules. Not advice. Just things I've come to believe — slowly, through experience, through being wrong, through watching people I admire. Subject to change. Always.

I'm twenty years old. Everything I believe is provisional. I'm writing this not because I have answers but because naming what you believe forces you to actually believe it — and I think that's worth doing even when you're still figuring it out.

01
Curiosity is the most important thing you can be.
Not smart. Not hardworking. Curious. Everything else follows from being genuinely interested in how things work and why people are the way they are.
02
Most growth happens in rooms nobody else is in.
The quiet moments of working through something hard, being wrong and correcting it, choosing to try again — that's where you actually become someone. No audience required.
03
Depth beats breadth, almost every time.
Knowing one thing deeply — really deeply — is more valuable than knowing many things shallowly. The world is full of people who know a little about a lot. Fewer know a lot about something.
04
Consistency is more powerful than intensity.
One good hour every day beats ten spectacular hours once a month. This is the thing I believe most clearly and practice most poorly.
05
The quality of your attention is the quality of your life.
Where you put your attention is where your life goes. This sounds simple and is actually very hard. Most of us give our best attention to things that don't deserve it.
06
Being kind costs almost nothing and changes a lot.
Not performatively kind. Actually kind — in the small, unspectacular ways that make someone's day slightly better. This is underrated as a value and underrated as a skill.
07
You don't need to have it figured out to start.
Ready is largely a myth. Most people who did something meaningful started before they felt qualified. The figuring out happens during, not before.
08
Your relationship with yourself is the longest one you'll have.
Worth investing in. Worth being honest in. Worth showing up for — especially on the days when being someone else would be easier.

Things I'm still figuring out —

Honest questions I don't have answers to yet. Listed here so I don't pretend otherwise.

—How to want things badly and be okay if they don't happen. Both at once.
—What the difference is between patience and giving up. It changes depending on the day.
—How to rest without guilt. Still working on this one.
—Whether ambition and contentment can coexist or if you have to keep choosing one.
—How to miss people without letting it hollow you out.
—What I actually want — not what I think I should want, not what looks good, but actually want.
