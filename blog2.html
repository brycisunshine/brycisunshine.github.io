A Soul Caught Between
Dreams and Reality

March 3, 20269 min read

There are certain people who live two lives at once.

One life is visible to everyone else — the practical one. The life of schedules, obligations, conversations, responsibilities, and expectations. It is the life where they do what needs to be done, respond when spoken to, keep moving, and appear normal enough to the outside world.

But the second life is hidden.

It exists in their mind, in their heart, in late-night thoughts and private hopes. It is the life made of ambition, imagination, questions, longing, and the quiet belief that something greater is still possible. This unseen life is where they dream of becoming more than their current circumstances, more than what others assume, more than what fear has allowed so far.

"People aren't home, they never will be. People are rivers — always changing, forever flowing. They will disappear with everything you put inside them."

Living between these two worlds is not easy. This person often feels the tension of wanting more while still being tied to where they are now. They know they are capable of growth, but growth can feel slow. They know they have potential, but potential can feel frustrating when it remains unrealized. They know they want change, yet change asks for courage, patience, and sacrifice.

So they keep carrying both realities. They handle everyday life while secretly imagining better days. They manage responsibilities while planning future victories no one knows about. They smile in conversations while fighting self-doubt internally. They appear calm while wrestling storms inside.

"Kitna bhi kuch karlu pagle, end me tu yaad aa hi jata hain."

— the kind of thought that arrives uninvited at midnight

People may underestimate them because they do not always announce their intentions. But silence should never be mistaken for emptiness. Inside them is movement. Inside them are plans forming, lessons being learned, strength being built.

This person has likely known disappointment. Dreams delayed. Efforts unnoticed. Moments when they wondered if trying was worth it. Times when watching others succeed felt heavy. Times when they questioned whether they were late, lost, or lacking.

Yet they are still here.

That matters more than it seems. Because continuing after discouragement is its own kind of greatness. Holding onto vision while reality feels ordinary is its own kind of courage. Refusing to give up on yourself, even quietly, is its own kind of victory.

"I think I miss that one friend I used to share everything with. You were my safe zone."

— and sometimes that's the loss that hits the hardest

Their life story is not about someone who had everything figured out early. It is about someone learning, adapting, healing, striving, and slowly turning inner dreams into outer reality.

And one day, people may call their success sudden.

But only they will know how many invisible years it took.
