Books

Books that
rewired how I think.

I don't read fast. I read slowly and argue with the pages. These are the ones that won.

Thinking, Fast and Slow
Daniel Kahneman
Completely changed how I understand my own decisions. The chapter on loss aversion alone was worth the whole book.
Psychology · Non-fiction
The Almanack of Naval Ravikant
Eric Jorgenson
I've come back to this more than any other book. It's not self-help. It's something quieter and more honest than that.
Philosophy · Life
Atomic Habits
James Clear
The 1% better every day idea sounds obvious until you actually apply it and watch what happens over months.
Productivity · Behaviour
Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman
Richard Feynman
The most convincing argument I've ever read for being genuinely, unashamedly curious about everything.
Science · Memoir
The Alchemist
Paulo Coelho
Read it at exactly the right time. The idea that the universe conspires in your favour when you really want something — I've thought about that a lot.
Fiction · Philosophy
Man's Search for Meaning
Viktor Frankl
The most important book I've read about what makes a life worth living. Short. Dense. Stays with you.
Psychology · Philosophy
